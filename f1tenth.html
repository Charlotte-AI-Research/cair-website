F1Tenth

Autonomous miniature racecar for high-speed navigation

Overview

F1Tenth is our high-speed autonomous racing project that challenges students to develop algorithms for real-time decision making and navigation at racing speeds. Using 1/10th scale race cars, teams compete in autonomous racing competitions while advancing the state-of-the-art in autonomous vehicle technology.

The project emphasizes rapid sensor processing, path planning under uncertainty, and control systems that can handle high-speed maneuvers. Students work with LIDAR sensors, IMUs, and advanced control algorithms to create vehicles capable of navigating complex tracks at maximum speed while avoiding obstacles and competing against other autonomous vehicles.

Requirements

Background in Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, or Mechanical Engineering
Experience with control systems and real-time programming
Knowledge of SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)
Programming skills in Python and C++
Interest in autonomous vehicles and competitive racing

Get Involved

Ready to join the exciting world of autonomous racing? Our F1Tenth team is always looking for passionate students who want to push the boundaries of autonomous vehicle technology. Whether you're interested in sensor fusion, path planning, control systems, or competitive racing, there's a role for you on our team. We participate in national F1Tenth competitions and provide hands-on experience with cutting-edge autonomous vehicle technologies.
